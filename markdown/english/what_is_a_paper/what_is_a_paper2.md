<div class="ps-block ps-callout">
  <div class="ps-callout-title">
    <i class="bi bi-lightbulb-fill"></i>
    Main Concept
  </div>
    By convention, scientific papers are written in an objective and impersonal style. This can give the impression that the findings presented are absolute truths, entirely absent of human intervention, and therefore absent of human error or fallibility.
</div>

<div class="ps-divider"></div>

## Common Misconceptions

<div class="ps-myths">

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        Published research is <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">objective truth</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        A paper is a structured report of one study. It reflects specific methods, assumptions, samples, and analytical decisions made by human researchers.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        If a study is well designed, its conclusions are <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">universally true</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        Even a perfectly executed study only tells you what happened under those specific conditions, with that specific sample.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        Widely cited studies must be <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">beyond criticism</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        Highly cited research can still contain limitations, hidden biases, questionable assumptions, or conclusions that stretch beyond the data.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

<div class="ps-divider"></div>

<div class="ps-plain">
  <div class="ps-plain-title">
    <i class="bi bi-translate"></i>
    In plain English
  </div>
  Papers contain evidence, but it is up to you to evaluate how strong that evidence is.
</div>

</div>
