  <div class="ps-callout-title">
    <i class="bi bi-lightbulb-fill"></i>
    Main Concept
  </div>
    A statistically significant result does not automatically mean an effect is large, important, causal, or even reliable.
</div>

<div class="ps-divider"></div>

## Common Misconceptions

<div class="ps-myths">

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        Statistical significance means the result is <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">important</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        Statistical significance only tells you the result is unlikely under the null hypothesis.
        It does not tell you whether the effect is large, meaningful, or practically relevant.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        A non-significant result means there is <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">no effect</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        A non-significant result may reflect low power, high variability, or a small sample size. It does not prove the absence of an effect.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        Correlation implies <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">causation</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        An association between two variables does not establish a causal relationship.
        Confounding variables or reverse causation may explain the pattern.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ps-myth">
    <div class="ps-myth-icon"><i class="bi bi-x-circle-fill"></i></div>
    <div class="ps-myth-body">
      <div class="ps-myth-title">
        A large sample guarantees <span class="ps-highlight-wrong">good science</span>.
      </div>
      <div class="ps-myth-text">
        Large samples increase precision, but they do not fix biased sampling, poor design, or incorrect assumptions.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

</div>

<div class="ps-divider"></div>

<div class="ps-plain">
  <div class="ps-plain-title">
    <i class="bi bi-translate"></i>
    In plain English
  </div>
  Statistical tools help us quantify uncertainty. They do not replace rigorous study design, critical thinking, or thoughtful interpretation.
</div>
